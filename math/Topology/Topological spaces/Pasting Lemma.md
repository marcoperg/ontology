Let $X$ be a [[Topological spaces|topological space]] and $X = A \cup B$ with $A, B$ closed in $X$.

Let $f:A \to Y$ and $g : B\to Y$ be continuous maps such that $f(x) = g(x)$ for all $x \in A\cap B$.
Then  $h: X \to Y$
$$
h(x) = \begin{cases}
f(x) & \text{if } x\in A \\
g(x) & \text{if } x\in B
\end{cases}
$$
is well-defined and continous.
