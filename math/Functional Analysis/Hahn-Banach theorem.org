#+title: Hahn-Banach theorem
#+SETUPFILE: ../../setup.org

In this note $(X, \Vert \cdot \Vert)$ is a normed vector space over $\mathbb{R}$.

* Theorem statement

Let $Y \subset X$ be a subspace and $l: Y \to \mathbb{R}$ a linear functional. Then, if $p: X\to R$ is

1. Positive homogenious: $\forall x\in X, \alpha > 0\, p(\alpha x) = \alpha p(x)$.
2. Sub-additive: $p(x+y) \le p(x) + p(y)$ for all $x,y \in X$.
   
such that $l(x) \le p(x)$, $\forall x\in Y$, then there exists $L: X\to R$ such that
1. $L$ is linear.
2. $L(y) = l(y)$ for all $y\in Y$.
3. $L(x) \le p(x)$  for all $x\in X$.
