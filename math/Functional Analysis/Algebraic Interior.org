#+title: Algebraic interior
#+SETUPFILE: ../../setup.org

Given a normed vector space $(X, \Vert, \cdot, \Vert)$. Let $S \subset X$ be a subset. Then, $x\in S$ is set to be an *algebraic interior point of $S$* if $\forall y\in X, \exists \epsilon_y >0: x+ty \in S$ for all $|t|<\epsilon_y$.
