#+title: Minkowski inequality

Le $S$ be a [[id:75B6E188-5E38-40F9-85F1-EC44C3A12D0F][Measure space]], and $L^p(S)$ be the [[id:6685FCEC-5551-44BF-9E23-50CD4F65DEFC][Lp space]] from $S$.

\begin{theorem}
Let $1\lep\le\infty$ and let $f,g \in L^P(S)$. Then $f+g$ in $L^p(S)$, and we have the triangle inequality
$$\Vert f+g\Vert_p \le \Vert f\Vert_p + \Vert g\Vert_p$$

with equality for $1<p<\infty$ if and only if $f$ and $g$ are positively linearly depdent, i.e. $f=\lambda g$ for some $\lambda \ge 0$ or $g=0$.
\end{theorem}
