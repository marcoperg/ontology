#+title: Strong and weak topologies
#+SETUPFILE: ../../../setup.org

Let $X, Y$ be [[id:29768ACE-BD6A-47E1-9E63-EAFAEDB474AA][Banach spaces]], we consider the space $\mathscr{L}(X,Y)$ of [[id:D954F73B-E6C1-4000-8520-165EFB07B39C][bounded linear operators]] from $X$ to $Y$. We define three different topologies on this space.

* Uniform topology

For $M\in\mathscr{L}(X,Y)$ we define
$$\Vert M\vert= \sup_{\substack{x \in x\\ \|x\| = 1}} \Vert Mx\Vert.$$

* Strong topology
For $x\in X$ define
\begin{aligned}
\Gamma_x: \mathscr{L}(X,Y) &\to Y$$\\
M&\mapsto Mx
\end{aligned}
The strong topology is the coarsest topology for whichc $\Gamma_x$ is continuous for all $x\in X$.

* Weak topology
For $x\in X$, $l\in Y^*$ define

\begin{aligned}
\Gamma_{x, l}: \mathscr{L}(X,Y) &\to \mathbb{K}$$\\
M&\mapsto l(Mx)
\end{aligned}
The strong topology is the coarsest topology for whichc $\Gamma_{x,l}$ is continuous for all $x\in X, l\in Y^&$.
