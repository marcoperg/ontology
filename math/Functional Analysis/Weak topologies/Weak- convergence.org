#+title: Weak^* convergence
#+SETUPFILE: ../../../setup.org

Let $M$ be a [[id:29768ACE-BD6A-47E1-9E63-EAFAEDB474AA][Banach space]], let $X = M^*$ the continuous dual space to $M$. We can embed $M$ in
\[M^{**} = X^*\]. For each $m\in M$ define $L_m\in M^{**}$ as $L_m: M^*\to \mathbb{K}: x\mapsto x(m)$.


* Definition

Let $M, X=M^*$ be Banach spaces. We say that a sequence $(x_n)_{n\in\mathbb{N}}$ in $X$ weak^*ly converges to $x\in X$ if for all $m\in M$ $L_m(x_n) \rightarrow L_m(x)$. We denote weak^* convergence by $x_n \xrightharpoonup[]{w^*} x$.

Weak^* is weaker than [[id:DDA11B2B-893E-4AC3-AF11-E5135CBD766B][weak convergence]]. If $M$ is reflexive, then both notions are equivalent.
