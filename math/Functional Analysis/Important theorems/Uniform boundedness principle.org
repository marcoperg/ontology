#+title: Uniform boundedness principle
#+SETUPFILE: ../../setup.org

* General theorem for Banach spaces

Let $X$ be a [[id:29768ACE-BD6A-47E1-9E63-EAFAEDB474AA][Banach space]], $Y$ a normed vector spaced and $B(X, Y)$ the space of all continuous linear operators from $X$ into $Y$. Suppose that $F$ is a collection of continuous linear opereators from $X$ to $Y$. If, for every $x\in X$,
$$\sup_{T\in F} \Vert T(x) \Vert_Y < \infty$$

then

$$\sup_{T\in F} \Vert T\Vert_{B(X,Y)} < \infty.$$


* Theorem for complete metric spaces

Let $X$ be a complete [[id:09723E35-01DA-4F26-B3BD-B3CF4C94EBDC][metric space]]. Let $(f_\alpha: X \to \mathbb{R})_{\alpha\in I}$ a famility of continuous functions such thtat

$$\forall x\in X: \exists M_x < \infty : \sup_{\alpha\in I} | f_\alpha(x)| \le M_x$$

then there exists an open set $G$ and finite constants $c$ such that
$$\forall x\in G: \sup_{\alpha\in I} |f_\alpha(x)| < c.$$
