#+title: Pauli Group

The Pauli group \(G_1\) on 1 qubits is the 16-element matrix [[id:48FAF6F8-DD4A-49F5-89B1-DCE13AE4A2F7][group]] consisting of the \(2\times2\) identity matrix \(I\) and all of the Pauli matrices
\[
X = \sigma_1 = 
    \begin{bmatrix}
        0 & 1 \\
        1 & 0
    \end{bmatrix},
\quad
Y = \sigma_2 =
    \begin{bmatrix}
        0 & -i \\
        i & 0
    \end{bmatrix},
\quad
Z = \sigma_3 =
    \begin{bmatrix}
        1 & 0 \\
        0 & -1 \\
    \end{bmatrix}
\]
together with the products of these matrices with the factos \(\pm1\) and \(\pm i\):

\[G_1 := \{\pm I, \pm iI, \pm X, \pm iX, \pm Y, \pm iY, \pm Z, \pm iZ\} \equiv \langle X, Y, Z \rangle\]
The Pauli Group on \(n\) qubits \(G_n\), is the group generated by the operators describe above applied to each of \(n\) qubits in the [[id:1B6368CE-EB75-4424-A202-2E5A45E9C629][tensor product]] [[id:E5B1D38D-297B-4867-91DA-75156F747A92][Hilbert Space]] \((\mathbb{C}^2)^{\otimes n}\).
